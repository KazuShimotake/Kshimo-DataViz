Project 1: Manga Visualization

Intro:

Hello, some data visualizations have strict purposes. I am just ccoding up some visualizations just for my own personal enjoyment. And what something else I enjoy? Reading manga. So I decided to look at some data regarding the best selling manga as of 2023.

Problem Domain: List of best-selling manga

Manga - Japanese graphic novels that style developed around 19th Century Japan. - Wiki Manga-Wiki. Also, manga is the book form where as anime is animation or the video form. For more info: Beginners guide to manga

Serialization - For a manga to be serialized it means the series is now getting support from a publisher. Manga are usually serialized in these manga magazines.

Demographic - manga is sorted first by age group then genre, these are some of the common demongraphics:

  • Shonen - middle school boys,
    • Themes: Action, Adventure, Friendship, Coming of Age
  • Shojo - middle school girls,
    • Themes: Romance, Drama , Coming of Age
  • Seinan - adult men
    • Themes: Action Violence, Sex, Adult themes
  • Josei - adult women
    • Themes: Romance, Sex, Adult themes
  • Kodomomuke (Kodomo) - young children

Data Set: Best Selling Manga - D RAHULSINGH

Best Selling Manga

I found this data set on Kaggle and its basically the most popular manga based on its cumulative Sales for its Manga series. For this data set, these are the attributes:

  • Manga series
  • Author(s)
  • Publisher
  • Demographic
  • No. of collected volumes
  • Serialization
  • Approximate sales in million(s)
  • Average sales per volume in million(s)

Modification: For this manga the serialization given was a range. So we added two extra colums for the serialization start date and its end date. For the manga on hiatus or on going we put the end date as 2023.

Limitation: For this dataset, we do not have the sales for each year, we have only one point of reference. The last update for this data set occured in 2023. So this is a snapshot of the manga sales in 2023. This limitation impacted the data viusalizations since we can not graph the data over time, but rather try to see if there are any patterns amoung the other attributes for one point in time. For more of a sense of progressing and history, the best we can do is to look at the serialization start dates or look at the duration.

Tasks:

Original tasks:

  • Locate which mangas are sold the most over time
  • Locate which publisher makes the most sales
  • Compare the average sales per volume for different authors
  • Compare the sales between different mangas serialization status
  • Analyze the ratio between how long series has been out and its total sales

Tasks for Visualization:

  • Search for the# of mangas that started in each year
  • Sales of the manga over their lifetime ( sales vs serialization duration)

Visualizations:

Histogram

This histogram illustrates how many manga started the same year, where each bucket width is set to only contain one year. The color is used to show the proportion of demographics to each other for all the manga with the same start serialization.

  • Marks: lines
  • Channels: length of the bar, color

Pros:

  • Can see what demographics are most prevalent
  • Can see the number of Manga that started in a given year

Cons:

  • Have to keep track of the tops and bottoms of the bar for the exact number of a demographic
  • Sales of the manga over their lifetime ( sales vs serialization duration)

Heat Map

This heatmap is a varation on the same tast used for the hisotgram. One thing about the histogram was that it was hard to see the exact numbers of the demographics per year.

  • Marks: area
  • Channels: color hue, position of marks

Pros:

  • can see a general distribution of the Demographics over the serialization start date
  • can search for the exact number of each Demographic

Cons:

  • Cannot see what manga is what point. There are no labels
  • need to add up all the squares in the same column to get the total number of manga
  • Not good for getting exact quanities.

Scatter Plot

This is to see how the duration of a manga serialization effects its aproximate Sales. The dots are the unlabeled manga series, and the colors represent the demographic.

  • Marks: points
  • Channels: color, position of dot

Pros:

  • can see a general distribution of the Sales over duration

Cons:

  • Cannot see what manga is what point. There are no labels
  • Cannot see the start date for the Mangas, so no way to know if a manga lasted for 6 year if it was 6 years in the 1980s or 6 years in 2000s.
  • Not good for getting exact quanities.
